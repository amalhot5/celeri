Our Mission

Utilizing drone technology to deliver rapid access healthcare solutions.

Overdoses killed over 64,000 people last year according to the CDC, a 20% increase from the year before. Baltimore alone contributed 694 reported deaths due to overdoses in 2016, which is over twice the number of homicides reported in Baltimore county.

The most common, and yet the most dangerous symptom of an opioid overdose is depressed breathing. At three minutes, neurons will have suffered extensive damage, and lasting brain damage is very likely. At 5 minutes, death is imminent, and at 10 minutes, a coma and permanent brain damage is unavoidable.

Our Drone Technology

Reducing delivery times by over 50%

Our system consists of two parts. The first is the drone itself. It has a 20 minute fly-time, giving it a 10-mile radius of operability. The second is Headquarter, a SaaS style backend that acts as a headquarters for all of their drones. It will provide the user with the ability to send the drones out on missions, an overview of all the drones, e.g. their location, status, as well as allowing first responders to take control of flight, and giving them access to a live-feed from a specific drone. This system will allow counties to send cheaper ambulances and have less support staff for overdoses.

Naloxone

The opioid overdose-reversal drug

Narcan has developed a nasal spray applicator for naloxone, making it easier than ever before to administer. Having absolutely no side effects, there is no risk if incorrectly administered or administered to the wrong person. This makes it an incredibly easy and safe drug for any bystander to administer. With a 93% success rate, this is an overdose victim's best chance at survival.
